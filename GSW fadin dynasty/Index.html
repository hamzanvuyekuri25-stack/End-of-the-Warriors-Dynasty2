End of the warriors dynasty

"We are no longer the 17 warriors dominating the league, We are a fading dynasty"~ Steve Kerr

The Golden State Warriors had a mixed but competitive season, showing flashes of their championship pedigree while also dealing with inconsistency. Led by Stephen Curry, the team remained dangerous offensively, with strong three-point shooting and veteran leadership keeping them in playoff contention. However, defensive lapses, injuries, and an overreliance on their core players at times limited their overall dominance. Younger players showed gradual development, but not always enough to fully support the veterans. Overall, the Warriors stayed relevant in the Western Conference, proving they can still compete, though not quite at the dominant level of their peak years.

During this season, injuries to Stephen Curry had a direct and often immediate impact on the Golden State Warriors, particularly in stretches where he missed multiple games. The team’s offensive rating typically dipped without his elite shooting and off-ball movement, leading to less spacing and more predictable possessions. Ball-handling duties shifted to other guards, but the overall efficiency and late-game execution often suffered. While some players stepped up in short bursts, the Warriors struggled to maintain consistency over longer periods without Curry, which affected their seeding and momentum across the season.

The Golden State Warriors this season have looked far less dominant compared to their remarkable 2017 campaign. In 2017, led by stars like Stephen Curry, Kevin Durant, Klay Thompson, and Draymond Green, the team displayed near-perfect chemistry, elite defense, and one of the most efficient offenses in NBA history, culminating in a dominant championship run. By contrast, this season’s Warriors have been more inconsistent, dealing with injuries, roster changes, and less defensive cohesion, which has made them competitive but not overwhelming. While they still show flashes of brilliance, especially through Curry’s leadership, they lack the depth, stability, and two-way dominance that defined their 2017 peak.
